Earth Overshoot Day

Earth Overshoot Day marks the date when humanity's demand for ecological resources and services in a given year exceeds what Earth can regenerate in that year.

What is Earth Overshoot Day?

Earth Overshoot Day is calculated by dividing the planet's biocapacity (the amount of ecological resources Earth is able to generate that year) by humanity's Ecological Footprint (humanity's demand for that year), and multiplying by 365, the number of days in a year:

(Earth's Biocapacity / Humanity's Ecological Footprint) × 365 = Earth Overshoot Day

When this day falls earlier in the year, it means we're consuming resources faster and operating at a greater ecological deficit.

Understanding Overconsumption

Overconsumption is the excessive consumption or use of goods and services (energy, land, water or materials) that cause harm or detrimental effects to humans and/or the environment, namely by exceeding the carrying capacity and life supporting systems of the planet and its ecosystems.

Earth Overshoot Day marks the date when humanity has used all the biological resources that Earth regenerates during the entire year.

What This Means

In 2024, Earth Overshoot Day fell on August 1st (day 214). This means that humanity used as many ecological resources as Earth can regenerate in the entire year by the 214th day of the year.

For the rest of the year, we operate in ecological deficit, depleting resources and accumulating waste (primarily carbon dioxide in the atmosphere).

Moving the Date

The goal is to #MoveTheDate of Earth Overshoot Day back. If we moved the date back by 5 days each year, we would return to using the resources of less than one planet by 2050.

Human economies and societies depend on the health of our planet. Living within the means of one Earth is not only environmentally necessary, but economically beneficial in the long run.
